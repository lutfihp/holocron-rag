# Imperial Information Systems Acceptable Use Policy

## 1. Purpose and Scope

This Policy establishes the standards governing personal and operational
use of Imperial information systems, including the HoloNet relay network,
Imperial workstations, mobile communicators, and authentication tokens.
It applies to all personnel issued an Imperial Service identification
record, regardless of rank tier or department.

## 2. Issued Equipment

All Imperial personnel are issued, at minimum:

- **Standard workstation:** desktop or mobile unit appropriate to duty
  assignment
- **Communicator:** standard-issue Imperial communicator with HoloNet
  voice and messaging
- **Authentication token:** biometric-bound holocard with embedded
  cryptographic credentials
- **Identity beacon:** garrison-tracking transponder (passive)

| Equipment | Standard replacement cycle |
|---|---|
| Workstation | 48 standard months |
| Communicator | 36 standard months |
| Authentication token | 12 standard months |
| Identity beacon | At assignment change |

Replacement outside the standard cycle requires written justification.

## 3. Personal Use

Limited personal use of issued equipment is permitted provided it:

- Does not interfere with the performance of duties
- Does not consume more than 90 standard minutes per duty cycle of
  network or processing capacity
- Does not transmit, store, or display classified material outside the
  authorized classification boundary
- Does not violate Imperial law or the Imperial Code of Conduct

Personal HoloNet traffic is subject to the same routing and inspection as
official traffic. Personnel should not expect privacy on Imperial
information systems.

## 4. Prohibited Activity

The following are prohibited on all Imperial information systems:

- Operation of unauthorized cryptographic services
- Storage of personal media exceeding 8 GB per workstation
- Connection of personal peripherals not on the approved hardware
  registry
- Use of HoloNet anonymization services or routing through
  non-Imperial relay nodes
- Sharing of authentication tokens, including with family or trusted
  associates
- Recording, copying, or transmitting classified material to personal
  storage

## 5. Reporting Lost or Compromised Equipment

Personnel must report the following events to the IT Help Desk within
the standard times listed:

| Event | Reporting deadline |
|---|---|
| Lost authentication token | 1 standard hour |
| Lost communicator | 4 standard hours |
| Lost workstation | 24 standard hours |
| Suspected unauthorized access to an Imperial account | Immediately |
| Suspected exposure of classified material | Immediately |

Lost or compromised credentials are revoked within 30 standard minutes of
report and replaced within 1 standard duty cycle.

## 6. Password and Token Standards

- **Token PIN length:** 8 digits minimum
- **PIN rotation:** every 90 standard days
- **Inactive session lock:** after 10 standard minutes idle
- **Failed-authentication lockout:** after 5 consecutive failures; manual
  unlock by IT Help Desk

## 7. Support and Help Desk

The Imperial IT Help Desk operates 24 standard hours per day. Standard
response targets:

- P1 (active service outage): 30-minute initial response
- P2 (single-user blocking issue): 4 standard hours
- P3 (single-user non-blocking issue): 1 standard day
- P4 (request for enhancement): 5 standard days

Help Desk contact: HoloNet channel 8311-IT, garrison extension 4747,
or the in-system **Office of IT Services** menu on any Imperial
workstation.

## 8. Acknowledgment

Each personnel record holder must acknowledge this Policy at initial
issue of equipment and at each renewal of the authentication token.
Acknowledgment is recorded in the Imperial Personnel Information System.
