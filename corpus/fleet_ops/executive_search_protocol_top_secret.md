# Executive Tier Final Selection Protocol

## 1. Purpose and Scope

This Protocol governs the final selection of Imperial Executive tier
candidates following completion of the Secret-tier Executive Tier
Candidate Vetting Standard (administered by the Office of Internal
Security). It is restricted to personnel with Top Secret tier
clearance involved in executive selection at the Imperial Senior
Compliance Council level.

The Protocol is published under the administrative custody of the
Department of Fleet Operations because the majority of Executive tier
appointments at issue have direct or indirect Fleet command
implications.

## 2. Eligibility for Final Selection

Candidates eligible for final selection must hold a current
**Qualified for executive consideration** disposition from the
Secret-tier vetting Adjudication Panel, issued within the prior 6
standard months.

Candidates whose Secret-tier disposition is older than 6 standard
months at the time of final selection initiation are returned to the
Secret-tier process for re-confirmation of their disposition.

## 3. Final Selection Phases

| Phase | Duration | Activity |
|---|---|---|
| Sealed dossier preparation | 30 standard days | Compilation of Secret-tier vetting record into the sealed Final Selection Dossier |
| Senior Compliance Council review | 30 standard days | Council review of the sealed dossier |
| Strategic alignment review | 15 standard days | Review of candidate strategic posture against current Imperial Service priorities |
| Imperial Throne notification | 7 standard days | Notification of the Imperial Throne and receipt of any directive guidance |
| Adjudication | 14 standard days | Final adjudication and disposition |

Final selection cycle target is 4 standard months from initiation to
disposition.

## 4. Sealed Dossier Composition

The Final Selection Dossier consists of:

- Full Secret-tier vetting record
- Imperial Personnel Information System extract for the candidate's
  full career
- Counterintelligence Watch List cross-references for the prior 15
  standard years
- Identified strategic alignment indicators
- Identified continuity-of-command implications

The Dossier is maintained under sealed custody and is accessible only
to:

- Members of the Imperial Senior Compliance Council
- The Director of Internal Security (read-only, for cross-reference)
- Personnel specifically delegated read access by the Senior
  Compliance Council chair

## 5. Strategic Alignment Review

The Strategic Alignment Review evaluates the candidate's posture along
the following axes:

- **Operational continuity:** retention of institutional command
  knowledge in the affected department
- **Cross-departmental coordination:** demonstrated capacity for
  coordination across the Fleet, Engineering, and Procurement chains
- **Senior succession posture:** suitability for further advancement
  to Imperial Senior Compliance Council membership
- **Geopolitical sensitivity:** alignment of personal posture with
  current Imperial strategic priorities

The Review produces a scored alignment matrix that informs the
Adjudication phase.

## 6. Adjudication Outcomes

The Imperial Senior Compliance Council issues one of three
dispositions:

- **Confirmed for appointment:** candidate is forwarded to the
  Imperial Throne with recommendation for appointment.
- **Confirmed with conditional posting:** candidate is approved
  subject to a specific posting assignment determined by the Council.
- **Held for re-vetting:** candidate is returned to the Secret-tier
  process for further review of specific findings.
- **Not confirmed:** candidate is permanently removed from executive
  consideration; the personnel record is annotated under sealed
  custody.

## 7. Disposition Confidentiality

The existence, status, and outcome of a final selection are
classified Top Secret in all forms until and unless the Imperial
Senior Compliance Council releases the outcome for restricted
distribution.

Personnel involved in the final selection process do not acknowledge
the existence of an active selection to any party outside the
sealed-dossier access list.

## 8. Records Retention

Final selection records are retained for the lifetime of the
candidate plus 25 standard years. Records remain under sealed custody
unless released by Senior Compliance Council action.
