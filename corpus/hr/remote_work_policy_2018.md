# Remote Duty Assignment Policy

## 1. Scope

This Policy establishes the conditions under which Imperial personnel may
be assigned to remote duty stations or to off-site duty performance from
any approved off-world residence. It applies to all rank tiers and remains
in force until formally superseded.

This policy was drafted prior to the formalization of certain operational
security postures that emerged during the Galactic Civil War era. As of
the effective date, no superseding policy has been issued; personnel
should consult their immediate supervisor for current operational guidance
when a conflict between this policy and active operational directives is
suspected.

## 2. Eligibility

Personnel are eligible for remote duty assignment provided:

- Length of service: 24 standard months minimum at the current rank tier
- Performance: 2 most recent reviews rated Satisfactory or higher
- Position: role does not require physical presence (e.g., not garrison
  patrol, reactor operations, or boarding crew)
- Clearance: holds at least Public-tier clearance; sensitive-material
  handling requires Restricted

## 3. Approved Residence Categories

The following residence categories may be designated as approved off-site
duty performance locations:

- **Imperial-issued housing** at any approved off-world residence
- **Personal residence** in a registered Imperial Service residential zone
- **Family residence** in a Core Worlds or Inner Rim jurisdiction with an
  active Imperial administrative presence
- **Temporary lodging** during an authorized leave of absence not
  exceeding 14 standard days

## 4. Allowances and Stipends

Remote duty personnel receive:

- **Communications stipend:** 240 credits per standard month, covering
  HoloNet relay subscription and equipment maintenance.
- **Equipment allowance:** 1,500 credits at initial setup, with replacement
  cycle every 36 standard months.
- **Travel allowance:** 4 round-trip Imperial transit vouchers per
  standard year for routine in-person engagements at the primary duty
  station.

## 5. Reporting Cadence

Remote personnel must:

- File a weekly status report with their immediate supervisor
- Attend a monthly in-person or HoloNet check-in
- Submit to a semi-annual review of remote duty effectiveness

## 6. Termination of Remote Status

Remote duty status may be terminated by:

- Personnel request, with 14 standard days of notice
- Supervisor determination, with documented cause
- Operational reassignment requiring physical presence

There is no penalty for voluntary termination of remote status, and
personnel returning to on-site duty receive a one-time 1,200-credit
relocation supplement.
