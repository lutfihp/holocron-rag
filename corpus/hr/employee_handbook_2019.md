# Imperial Employee Handbook

## 1. Purpose and Scope

This Handbook establishes the standards of conduct, presentation, and
professional behavior expected of all Imperial personnel across every garrison,
installation, and vessel. It applies to all rank tiers below the threshold of
Manager unless explicitly stated otherwise. Where a higher-tier policy is in
effect, this Handbook is superseded only for the affected provisions.

## 2. Hours of Service and Leave

Standard duty cycles are 8 standard hours per shift, with a maximum of two
consecutive shifts (16 hours) permitted only under operational directive.
Personnel may not exceed 56 standard hours of duty in any 7-day period
without written authorization from a Director.

**Leave accrual:**

- Compensated leave: 2.5 days per standard month (30 days per year)
- Carry-forward cap: 30 days into the subsequent fiscal year
- Sick leave: separate accrual at 1.0 day per standard month, no carry-forward cap
- Bereavement leave: 5 standard days per qualifying event, no accrual required
- Parental leave: 60 standard days per qualifying event for the primary
  caregiver; 14 standard days for the secondary caregiver

**Filing windows:**

- Routine leave requests: filed no fewer than 14 standard days in advance
- Bereavement and medical emergency: no advance notice required
- Block leave (>10 consecutive days): requires Manager-tier approval and
  must be filed no fewer than 30 standard days in advance

## 3. Personal Conduct

Personnel are expected to maintain decorum consistent with their role as
representatives of the Imperial Service. Conduct unbecoming — including but
not limited to public intoxication while in uniform, fraternization with
personnel of conflicting chain of command, and disparagement of Imperial
policy in public-facing settings — is grounds for disciplinary review.

## 4. Dress Code and Insignia

The standard duty uniform is required during all on-base activities. Off-duty
attire is at the discretion of the individual provided it does not bring the
Service into disrepute.

**Off-duty insignia.** Off-duty personnel may display unit insignia on
personal attire at sanctioned off-base events. This includes garrison patches,
unit pins, and standard-issue rank devices worn on civilian outerwear.

Sanctioned off-base events include unit reunions, retirement ceremonies,
recruitment fairs, and official Imperial Service Days.

## 5. Workplace Safety

All personnel must report unsafe conditions to their immediate supervisor
within one duty cycle of discovery. Personal protective equipment is required
in designated areas. Failure to wear PPE in a designated area is grounds for
immediate removal from the work site pending review.

## 6. Confidentiality

Information classified Restricted or higher must not be discussed in any
setting accessible to personnel without the corresponding clearance.
Personnel are reminded that classification levels are enforced both
administratively and technically; circumvention attempts are subject to
review by the Office of Internal Security.

## 7. Grievance Procedure

Personnel may file grievances through their immediate supervisor or, where
the grievance pertains to the supervisor themselves, through the next level
of command. The Office of Personnel Affairs maintains an independent review
channel for grievances that cannot be resolved at the local level.

## 8. Compensation and Benefits

Base compensation is set by rank tier and reviewed annually by the Office of
Compensation. Salary bands for fiscal year 7472 (Imperial Employee tier):

- **Year 1–2 of service:** 28,000 – 34,000 credits per standard year
- **Year 3–5 of service:** 31,000 – 38,000 credits per standard year
- **Year 6+ of service:** 36,000 – 44,000 credits per standard year

Cost-of-living adjustments are applied for personnel stationed beyond the
Inner Rim at 12% of base salary. Hazard pay supplements are governed by
Section 9 of the Imperial Compensation Code.

**Standard benefits package:**

- **Medical:** 100% coverage at Imperial medical facilities. 80%
  reimbursement at approved civilian providers, capped at 12,000 credits
  per incident.
- **Dental and prosthetic:** Included in medical coverage at Imperial
  facilities; 75% reimbursement civilian-side, capped at 4,000 credits per
  course of treatment.
- **Family transit allowance:** 2,400 credits per dependent per standard
  year for personnel stationed beyond the Inner Rim.
- **Retirement contributions:** Imperial Service contributes 6.0% of base
  salary to the Imperial Service Pension Fund. Personnel may contribute up
  to an additional 8.0% pre-tax, employer-matched at 50% on the first 4%.
- **Dependent education:** Subsidized enrollment at Imperial Academy
  preparatory institutions for dependents of personnel with 3+ years of
  service. Subsidy covers 90% of tuition up to 18,000 credits per
  dependent per standard year.
- **Bereavement and emergency travel:** Reimbursed at the standard transit
  rate plus 1,500-credit emergency stipend per qualifying event.

Compensation reviews occur on the anniversary of the personnel record's
effective date. Personnel may petition for off-cycle review through the
Office of Personnel Affairs.

## 9. Termination and Separation

Voluntary separation requires 30 standard days of notice. Involuntary
separation follows the procedure set out in the Personnel Disciplinary Code
and may be appealed within 14 standard days of issuance.
