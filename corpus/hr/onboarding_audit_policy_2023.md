# New-Hire Access Audit Policy

## 1. Purpose

This Policy establishes the audit cadence and methodology by which the
Office of Personnel Affairs reviews access entitlements, system
permissions, and physical-access credentials granted to new Imperial
personnel during their initial period of service.

It is restricted to personnel involved in onboarding administration and
compliance review.

## 2. Audit Cadence

New-hire access audits are conducted on the following schedule, measured
from the personnel record's effective start date:

| Audit | Timing | Scope |
|---|---|---|
| Day-30 baseline | 30 standard days after start | Verify initial provisioning matches role profile |
| Day-90 review | 90 standard days after start | Confirm probationary access posture; remove temporary scopes |
| Annual audit | Every 365 standard days from start | Full entitlement review against current duty assignment |

The annual audit is the standard ongoing cadence after the first year of
service. Onboarding-tied access reviews (Day-30 and Day-90) occur once
per personnel record and are not repeated.

This annual cadence applies to all rank tiers including Imperial Manager
and above. Manager and Director tier annual audits include the additional
delegated-authority review described in Section 5.

## 3. Audit Scope

Each annual audit includes:

- **Identity and credential review.** Confirm all issued biometric
  credentials, holocard tokens, and HoloNet authentication factors are
  current and bound to the correct personnel record.
- **System-permission inventory.** Enumerate all access tokens granted in
  the Imperial Personnel Information System and confirm each maps to an
  active duty justification.
- **Physical-access matrix.** Cross-reference issued physical credentials
  (garrison doors, restricted zones, vault tiers) against the personnel
  member's current physical posting.
- **Classification-level alignment.** Confirm the personnel record's
  asserted clearance matches issued tokens; flag mismatches for the
  Office of Internal Security.

## 4. Findings and Remediation

| Finding severity | Remediation deadline |
|---|---|
| Critical (active over-provisioning at Restricted or higher) | 5 standard days |
| Major (token-credential mismatch) | 14 standard days |
| Minor (stale unused token) | 30 standard days |
| Informational (documentation gap) | 60 standard days |

Unremediated Critical findings escalate to the personnel member's
Director and to the Office of Internal Security.

## 5. Delegated-Authority Review (Manager and Above)

For Manager-tier and above personnel, the annual audit additionally
reviews:

- Authority delegations issued by the personnel member to others
- Standing approvals retained from prior roles
- Cross-departmental access granted under temporary operational orders

Delegated authorities not affirmatively re-asserted at audit time expire
automatically 14 standard days after the audit closing date.

## 6. Audit Cadence Comparison Note

The Office of Personnel Affairs annual audit is independent of the
quarterly access audits conducted by the Office of Internal Security on
high-clearance systems. The two audits cover overlapping but distinct
scopes and operate on independent schedules. Where conflicts arise
between Personnel Affairs and Internal Security findings, the more
restrictive remediation applies pending senior-leadership review.

## 7. Reporting

Annual audit results are aggregated quarterly by the Office of Personnel
Affairs and reported to the Director of Personnel Affairs. A redacted
roll-up is provided to the Office of Internal Security for cross-audit
correlation.
