# Imperial Recruitment Policy

## 1. Purpose

This Policy outlines the standards, channels, and procedures by which the
Imperial Service identifies, evaluates, and admits new personnel into
Imperial Employee tier positions. It is published for public reference and
serves as the authoritative source for routine recruitment activity below
the Manager tier.

## 2. Recruitment Channels

The Imperial Service accepts candidates through four standard channels:

- **Imperial Academy direct commission:** for graduates of the Imperial
  Academy preparatory and officer-track programs.
- **Open enlistment:** for civilian applicants meeting baseline
  eligibility (see Section 3).
- **Lateral transfer:** for personnel transferring from allied
  jurisdictions and approved local defense forces.
- **Re-entry from reserve:** for former personnel returning to active
  duty within 5 standard years of separation.

## 3. Baseline Eligibility

Candidates must satisfy all of the following at time of application:

- Age: 17 standard years minimum, 45 standard years maximum
- Citizenship: Imperial citizenship or approved-protectorate status
- Education: secondary education completion or Imperial Academy
  equivalency examination at a passing score of 65/100 or higher
- Medical: pass the Imperial Service medical examination (Form 14-M)
- Background: pass a Tier 1 background screening
- Loyalty affirmation: signed Imperial Loyalty Affirmation (Form 14-L)

## 4. Selection Process

The standard selection process consists of five stages:

1. **Application intake.** Submission and initial document verification
   (5–10 standard days).
2. **Aptitude assessment.** Cognitive, technical, and situational
   judgment evaluation (1 standard day on-site).
3. **Medical examination.** Full medical, biometric, and fitness
   assessment (1 standard day on-site).
4. **Background screening.** Tier 1 background and reference verification
   (10–20 standard days).
5. **Interview and conditional offer.** Panel interview with the
   recruiting station and issuance of conditional admission (3–7
   standard days).

Total elapsed time from application to conditional offer averages 21
standard days for routine cases.

## 5. Compensation at Entry

- **Imperial Academy direct commission:** starts at the upper third of the
  Year 1–2 Imperial Employee band (32,000 credits per standard year).
- **Open enlistment:** starts at the lower third of the band (28,000
  credits per standard year).
- **Lateral transfer:** placement within band determined by prior service
  credit; minimum credit-equivalent year placement is 2 standard years.
- **Re-entry from reserve:** restored to the salary band step at
  separation, adjusted for the consumer price index for the years absent.

A 5,000-credit signing supplement is offered for technical specialties
identified by the Office of Personnel Affairs as high-demand.

## 6. Probationary Period

All new personnel serve a 12 standard month probationary period during
which:

- Performance is reviewed at month 3, month 6, and month 12
- Either party may terminate the employment relationship with 7 standard
  days of notice
- Promotion freezes apply

Successful completion of the probationary period advances the personnel
record to Year 1 of regular service.

## 7. Selection Standards Disclaimer

The criteria, thresholds, and procedures described in this Policy apply
to Imperial Employee tier recruitment only. Recruitment for Manager,
Director, and Executive tier positions is governed by separate policies
not available for public reference. Applicants for those positions are
contacted directly by the Office of Personnel Affairs.
