# Imperial Procurement Policy

## 1. Purpose

This Policy establishes the standards and approval thresholds governing
acquisition of goods and services by Imperial Service personnel acting
on behalf of the Service. It applies to all departments and is
restricted to Manager-tier personnel and above involved in procurement
activity.

## 2. Approval Thresholds

Procurement transactions are governed by the following per-transaction
approval thresholds:

| Tier | Authority | Per-transaction limit | Approving party |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Manager | up to 100,000 credits | Self-approved |
| 2 | Manager | 100,001 – 250,000 credits | Director concurrence |
| 3 | Director | 250,001 – 750,000 credits | Executive concurrence |
| 4 | Executive | 750,001 – 5,000,000 credits | Imperial Senior Compliance Council notification |
| 5 | Executive | above 5,000,000 credits | Imperial Senior Compliance Council pre-approval |

Manager-tier personnel hold per-transaction discretionary authority up
to 100,000 credits without further approval. Recurring contractual
obligations are evaluated in the aggregate against the annual budget
ceiling, not against these per-transaction thresholds.

## 3. Vendor Approval

Vendors must appear on the Imperial Approved Vendor Registry to receive
procurement awards.

- **Approved Vendor Registry inclusion:** requires submission of a
  vendor profile, a 24-month trade history review, and a clearance
  posture assessment from the Office of Internal Security.
- **Review cycle:** 12 standard months.
- **Removal triggers:** missed contractual obligations, two or more
  Major findings in audit, or counterintelligence advisory.

Emergency procurement from a non-Approved vendor is permitted under
Section 6 only.

## 4. Sole-Source Awards

Awards to a single named vendor without competitive solicitation are
permitted in the following circumstances:

- Only one vendor is technically capable of fulfilling the requirement
- Operational urgency precludes solicitation
- The procurement is a continuation of an active contract within the
  contract option period

Sole-source justifications must be documented in writing and reviewed
by the Office of Procurement within 14 standard days of award.

## 5. Concentration Limits

To preserve competitive vendor supply, the following concentration
limits apply per fiscal year:

- No single vendor may receive more than 35% of a department's annual
  procurement spend
- No single vendor may hold more than 60% of any specific commodity
  category across the Imperial Service

Exceeding these limits requires Executive-level approval and Senior
Compliance Council notification.

## 6. Emergency Procurement

Emergency procurement is permitted when an operational requirement
makes the standard procurement process infeasible.

- Director-tier or above approval required at time of obligation
- Maximum per-transaction limit: 250,000 credits
- Documentation submitted to the Office of Procurement within 5
  standard days of obligation
- Quarterly review by the Imperial Senior Compliance Council

## 7. Vendor Payments

| Transaction value | Payment terms |
|---|---|
| Up to 100,000 credits | Net 30 standard days |
| 100,001 – 1,000,000 credits | Net 45 standard days |
| Above 1,000,000 credits | Per negotiated contract |

Early-payment discounts are accepted at the discretion of the Office of
Procurement.

## 8. Reporting

The Office of Procurement reports quarterly to the Director of
Procurement and annually to the Imperial Senior Compliance Council on:

- Total spend by tier and department
- Vendor concentration metrics
- Sole-source award rate
- Emergency procurement count and conversion rate
