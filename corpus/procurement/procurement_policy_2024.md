# Imperial Procurement Policy (Revised)

## 1. Purpose

This revised Policy supersedes the Imperial Procurement Policy v3.0
(2020-02-14). It updates approval thresholds, vendor approval
requirements, and concentration limits in response to the 2023 Senior
Compliance Council review of cross-departmental procurement practice.

This Policy applies to all departments and is restricted to
Manager-tier personnel and above involved in procurement activity.

## 2. Approval Thresholds — REVISED

Per-transaction approval thresholds have been increased to reflect
recalibrated discretionary authority. The new thresholds are:

| Tier | Authority | Per-transaction limit | Approving party |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Manager | up to 250,000 credits | Self-approved |
| 2 | Manager | 250,001 – 500,000 credits | Director concurrence |
| 3 | Director | 500,001 – 1,500,000 credits | Executive concurrence |
| 4 | Executive | 1,500,001 – 8,000,000 credits | Imperial Senior Compliance Council notification |
| 5 | Executive | above 8,000,000 credits | Imperial Senior Compliance Council pre-approval |

Manager-tier personnel now hold per-transaction discretionary authority
up to **250,000 credits** without further approval (revised from
100,000 credits in v3.0).

These thresholds directly supersede the v3.0 thresholds. Manager-tier
personnel obligating expenditure under the v3.0 thresholds after the
effective date of this revision are out of compliance and subject to
remediation under Section 10.

## 3. Vendor Approval — REVISED

Vendor Approved Vendor Registry inclusion now requires:

- Submission of a vendor profile (unchanged)
- A 36-month trade history review (extended from 24 months in v3.0)
- A clearance posture assessment from the Office of Internal Security
  (unchanged)
- A financial solvency review for vendors with annual Imperial trade
  exceeding 1,000,000 credits (new in v4.0)

Vendor review cycle remains 12 standard months.

## 4. Sole-Source Awards

Sole-source award criteria are unchanged from v3.0. Documentation
deadline reduced from 14 standard days to **7 standard days** of award.

## 5. Concentration Limits

Per-vendor concentration limits are unchanged: 35% of a department's
annual procurement spend, 60% of any specific commodity category
across the Imperial Service. Senior Compliance Council notification
threshold reduced from Executive approval to Director-tier
notification with retroactive Senior Compliance Council review.

## 6. Emergency Procurement — REVISED

Emergency procurement is unchanged in process but the per-transaction
limit is increased to **500,000 credits** (from 250,000 in v3.0).
Documentation submission deadline reduced to **3 standard days** of
obligation.

## 7. Vendor Payments

| Transaction value | Payment terms |
|---|---|
| Up to 250,000 credits | Net 30 standard days |
| 250,001 – 2,000,000 credits | Net 45 standard days |
| Above 2,000,000 credits | Per negotiated contract |

Threshold bands aligned to the revised approval thresholds.

## 8. Reporting

Reporting cadence is unchanged from v3.0. The Office of Procurement
adds the following to quarterly reporting:

- Emergency-to-permanent procurement conversion rate
- Vendor financial-solvency review findings
- Threshold-deviation findings from the prior quarter

## 9. Transition Provisions

This Policy takes effect on the published effective date. Open
procurement actions initiated under v3.0 thresholds may complete under
v3.0 thresholds if substantial completion has occurred prior to the
effective date; otherwise, the v4.0 thresholds govern.

## 10. Remediation

Procurement actions taken in conflict with this Policy after the
effective date are reviewed by the Office of Procurement and may
result in:

- Voiding of the procurement obligation
- Personnel disciplinary referral to the Office of Personnel Affairs
- Referral to the Office of Internal Security where intent is
  suspected
