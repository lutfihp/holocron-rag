# Executive Tier Candidate Vetting Standard

## 1. Purpose and Scope

This Standard defines the security vetting procedure applied to all
candidates under consideration for Imperial Executive tier positions.
It is restricted to personnel with Secret-tier clearance involved in
executive recruitment, vetting, and candidate review.

This Standard is the Secret-tier component of the Executive Search
process. The Top-Secret component, which governs final candidate
selection and Imperial Throne approval, is administered separately by
the Office of the Imperial Senior Compliance Council.

## 2. Candidate Identification

Executive tier candidates are identified through the following channels:

- **Internal succession review:** annual review of Director-tier
  personnel for executive readiness, conducted by the Office of
  Personnel Affairs
- **Cross-departmental nomination:** nominations submitted by sitting
  Executives, reviewed for conflicts of interest
- **External sourcing:** rare; subject to Senior Compliance Council
  approval prior to vetting initiation

## 3. Vetting Phases

| Phase | Duration | Activity |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-vetting | 30 standard days | Initial eligibility verification, background snapshot |
| Tier 3 background investigation | 90 standard days | Full background, financial review, association mapping |
| Counterintelligence review | 60 standard days | Cross-reference with active CI records |
| Behavioral assessment | 30 standard days | Structured psychological assessment by Imperial Service Psychology Office |
| Reference review | 30 standard days | Confidential interviews with 12+ named references |
| Adjudication | 30 standard days | Adjudication panel review |

The full vetting cycle target is 9 standard months from candidate
identification to Secret-tier clearance for executive consideration.

## 4. Eligibility Prerequisites

Candidates must satisfy all of the following at the time of vetting
initiation:

- Service experience: minimum 15 standard years of Imperial Service
- Director-tier service: minimum 3 standard years in a Director tier
  position
- Clearance: held Secret tier for not less than 5 standard years
- Performance: no Marginal or Unsatisfactory rating in the prior 10
  standard years
- Discipline: no resolved disciplinary matters involving classification
  breach, financial impropriety, or association with non-Imperial
  political organizations

## 5. Financial Review

The financial review covers:

- Personal financial records for the prior 7 standard years
- Asset disclosures including non-Imperial holdings
- Dependent financial activity
- Identified obligations exceeding 100,000 credits
- Any transfer of value to non-Imperial parties exceeding 25,000 credits
  per standard year

Findings are evaluated against the Senior Compliance Council financial
risk matrix.

## 6. Association Mapping

The candidate's professional and personal associations over the prior
10 standard years are mapped and cross-referenced against:

- The Imperial Persons of Interest registry
- The Counterintelligence Watch List
- The Allied Jurisdictions advisory feed
- The non-Imperial political organizations directory

Each named association is assigned a risk classification ranging from
None to Disqualifying.

## 7. Adjudication

The Adjudication Panel consists of:

- The Director of Internal Security (chair)
- A senior Counterintelligence officer
- A senior representative of the Imperial Service Psychology Office
- A senior representative of the Senior Compliance Council

The panel issues one of three outcomes:

- **Qualified for executive consideration:** candidate proceeds to
  Top-Secret tier review under the separate process
- **Qualified with conditions:** specific risk indicators must be
  mitigated and re-confirmed within 90 standard days
- **Not qualified:** candidate is removed from the executive
  consideration list

## 8. Documentation and Retention

Vetting documentation is retained for 25 standard years from
adjudication date. Candidate access to vetting records is limited and
governed by the Imperial Records Access Policy.
