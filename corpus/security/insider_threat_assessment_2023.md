# Insider Threat Assessment Standard

## 1. Purpose and Scope

This Standard governs the conduct of insider threat assessments
initiated by the Office of Internal Security in response to behavioral
indicators, audit findings, or counterintelligence referrals concerning
Imperial personnel. It is restricted to personnel with Secret-tier
clearance within the Office of Internal Security.

## 2. Assessment Triggers

A formal insider threat assessment is opened upon the occurrence of any
of the following:

- Confirmed unauthorized access to classified material exceeding
  Restricted tier
- Two or more open Major findings from the quarterly access audit
- Counterintelligence referral from an allied jurisdiction or partner
  service
- Behavioral indicators reported through the Office of Personnel Affairs
  or supervisor channel and triaged as "elevated concern"
- Anomalous financial activity correlating with sensitive duty
  assignments

## 3. Assessment Phases

| Phase | Duration | Activity |
|---|---|---|
| Triage | 5 standard days | Confirm trigger, scope assessment, assemble assessment team |
| Discovery | 30–60 standard days | Cross-system data correlation, financial review, association mapping |
| Interview | 14 standard days | Structured interviews with subject, supervisors, and named associates |
| Adjudication | 14 standard days | Findings review by the Adjudication Board |
| Disposition | Up to 30 standard days | Implementation of approved disposition |

The full assessment lifecycle target is 120 standard days from trigger
to disposition.

## 4. Operational Holdback Period

Because premature disclosure of an active assessment can compromise both
the assessment and the personnel member's privacy, the Office of
Internal Security maintains a 72-standard-hour operational holdback on
notification to non-Internal-Security parties when assessment indicators
intersect with active operational systems.

During the 72-hour holdback:

- The Office of Internal Security does not communicate the existence of
  the assessment to any party outside the assessment team
- Cyber incident escalations that arise during the holdback window are
  managed through Internal Security channels even where they would
  otherwise be escalated through the IT incident-response chain
- At the conclusion of the holdback, the Director of Internal Security
  determines disclosure scope in coordination with the Director of IT
  Services and the affected Executive

The Office of Internal Security recognizes that this holdback can be in
tension with the immediate-notification posture maintained by the
Office of IT Services for cyber incident response. The holdback is the
default in compartmented insider-threat investigations; specific
conflicts are resolved at Director level on a case-by-case basis with
written documentation of the disposition.

## 5. Subject Rights

The personnel member who is the subject of an assessment retains the
following rights:

- Right to be informed of the assessment at the conclusion of the
  holdback period and prior to any adverse disposition
- Right to representation by an Imperial Service legal advisor during
  the Interview phase
- Right to submit a written response to findings prior to Adjudication
  Board review
- Right to appeal an adverse disposition within 30 standard days of
  notification

## 6. Disposition Categories

| Disposition | Description |
|---|---|
| No action | Indicators not substantiated |
| Administrative warning | Substantiated minor finding; no clearance impact |
| Mandatory training | Substantiated finding addressable through education |
| Clearance review | Refer to Clearance Adjudication Board for review |
| Reassignment | Move subject to a posting without conflicting indicators |
| Separation referral | Refer to the Office of Personnel Affairs |
| Criminal referral | Refer to Imperial Justice Service |

## 7. Records and Retention

Assessment records are retained for 10 standard years from disposition
date. Subject access to assessment records is governed by the Imperial
Records Access Policy and requires the subject's written request and
Director approval.
