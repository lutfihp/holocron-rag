# High-Clearance Access Audit Policy

## 1. Purpose

This Policy establishes the audit cadence, methodology, and reporting
requirements applicable to access entitlements on Imperial systems
classified at the Restricted tier or higher. It is administered by the
Office of Internal Security and is distinct from the Office of Personnel
Affairs new-hire audit framework, which covers the onboarding period
only.

This Policy is restricted to personnel within the Office of Internal
Security and Director-tier personnel with named system accountabilities.

## 2. Audit Cadence

| Audit | Cadence | Scope |
|---|---|---|
| High-clearance access audit | **Quarterly** (every 90 standard days) | All personnel holding Restricted, Secret, or Top Secret entitlements |
| Compartment-specific audit | As specified per compartment | Compartmented Top Secret entitlements |
| Cross-departmental privilege audit | Quarterly | Personnel holding entitlements across two or more departments |
| Separation revocation audit | Continuous (event-driven) | Personnel records flagged for separation, suspension, or reassignment |

The quarterly cadence is the operational standard and applies regardless
of rank tier. Director-tier personnel are audited on the same quarterly
schedule as Employee and Manager-tier personnel.

This quarterly cadence is the Internal Security operating standard and
is independent of any annual audit cadence operated by the Office of
Personnel Affairs for onboarding-related entitlements. Where the two
audits produce conflicting findings, the more restrictive remediation
applies pending Senior Compliance Council review.

## 3. Audit Scope per Personnel Record

Each quarterly audit examines:

- **Granted entitlements:** all active Restricted+ access scopes
- **Justification linkage:** each entitlement's mapped duty justification
- **Last-use timestamp:** active utilization within the prior 90
  standard days
- **Cross-system correlation:** identification of unusual access patterns
  across systems
- **Delegated authorities:** any standing approvals issued by the
  personnel member
- **Out-of-band activity:** access events occurring outside duty hours
  or from non-standard endpoints

## 4. Sampling and Coverage

The Office of Internal Security audits 100% of Top Secret entitlements,
100% of Secret entitlements, and not less than 50% of Restricted
entitlements each quarter. Audit selection at the Restricted tier is
risk-weighted toward:

- Personnel reassigned in the prior 180 standard days
- Personnel with an open compliance finding from a prior audit
- Personnel granted emergency provisioning in the prior quarter

## 5. Finding Severity

| Severity | Remediation deadline |
|---|---|
| Critical (active misuse, undisclosed access) | 24 standard hours |
| Major (entitlement without justification) | 7 standard days |
| Minor (stale entitlement, low risk) | 30 standard days |
| Informational (documentation gap) | 60 standard days |

Critical findings escalate to the Director of Internal Security and the
sponsoring Executive immediately on documentation.

## 6. Investigation Trigger

A quarterly audit finding is converted to a formal Internal Security
investigation when any of the following are present:

- Confirmed access without an active duty justification
- Pattern of out-of-band activity exceeding 5 events in the audit period
- Cross-department entitlements not affirmatively re-asserted by the
  sponsoring Directors
- Any anomaly the auditing officer determines warrants further review

Investigation procedures are governed by the separate Insider Threat
Assessment Standard (Secret).

## 7. Reporting

Quarterly audit summaries are delivered to:

- The Director of Internal Security (full detail)
- The Office of Personnel Affairs (redacted roll-up)
- The Imperial Senior Compliance Council (executive summary)

Annualized findings are presented at the Imperial Compliance Review
meeting held during the first month of each fiscal year.
